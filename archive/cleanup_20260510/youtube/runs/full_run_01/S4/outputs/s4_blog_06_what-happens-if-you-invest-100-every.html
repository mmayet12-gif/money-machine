**What Happens If You Invest $100 Every Month**

Imagine having an extra $100 each month to invest in your future. In this article, we'll delve into the world of monthly investing and show you how consistent efforts can add up over time.

**The Magic of Compounding**

Compound interest is the eighth wonder of the world. It's a mathematical concept that allows your investments to grow exponentially, often with little effort required from you. When you invest $100 in a high-interest savings account or a retirement fund, it earns interest on both the principal amount and any accrued interest.

Here's an example: if you deposit $1,000 into an account earning 5% annual interest, after one year you'll have approximately $1,050. But here's the magic part  in the second year, your new balance of $1,050 earns 5% interest on itself and the original principal, making it around $1,102.50.

This growth snowballs over time, resulting in significant wealth accumulation when you're consistent with your investments.

**Investment Scenarios**

Let's explore some real-life scenarios to put this concept into perspective:

*   **Emergency fund**: By investing $100 a month for five years, you'll have approximately $6,000 saved for emergencies or unexpected expenses.
*   **Retirement planning**: If you start saving $100 a month from age 25 and continue until 65, assuming an average annual return of 7%, you could have around $220,000 set aside for retirement.
*   **Short-term goals**: Investing small amounts regularly can help you reach short-term objectives, such as paying off debt or buying a car.

**Platform Choices**

With so many investment platforms available, selecting the right one can be overwhelming. Consider the following options when choosing where to invest your $100 each month:

1.  **High-yield savings accounts**: These accounts offer higher interest rates than traditional savings accounts and are FDIC-insured for up to $250,000.
2.  **Index funds or ETFs**: Diversified investments that track specific markets or sectors, often with lower fees than actively managed funds.
3.  **Micro-investing apps**: Platforms like Acorns or Stash allow you to invest small amounts into a diversified portfolio.

**Your First Investment**

Now that you're convinced of the power of monthly investing, it's time to take action. Here are some actionable steps to get you started:

1.  Set up automatic transfers from your checking account to your investment platform.
2.  Choose a mix of low-cost index funds or ETFs for long-term growth.
3.  Consider contributing to tax-advantaged accounts like 401(k) or IRA for retirement savings.
