**Title:** The Retirement Income Strategy That Never Runs Out

## Hook
Most Americans are going to outlive their retirement savings by a decade or more. The math is brutal — but the solutions are simple if you start applying them now.

## Context
Retirement planning is really about replacing your income with assets. When your investments generate as much as your job pays, work becomes optional. That's the goal.

## Act 1
Calculate your retirement number: multiply your annual expenses by 25. That's how much you need invested to safely withdraw 4% per year, indefinitely, adjusted for inflation. If you spend $60,000 per year, you need $1.5 million. Every $10,000 in annual spending adds $250,000 to the target.

## Act 2
Sequence of returns risk is the underrated retirement danger. If markets crash in your first five years of retirement and you're withdrawing 4%, you deplete your principal permanently. The counter-strategy: hold two to three years of expenses in cash or bonds. Never sell equities during a downturn.

## Act 3
The Roth vs traditional choice comes down to your current versus future tax rate. If you're in a low bracket now, pay taxes now with Roth contributions. If you're in a high bracket now, defer taxes with traditional contributions. Most people should do both — Roth IRA plus traditional 401K.

## Close
Retirement planning is simpler than the financial industry wants you to believe. Spend less than you earn, invest the difference consistently in low-cost index funds, and let time compound the results. The complexity gets added by people selling complexity. Subscribe for the streamlined version.
